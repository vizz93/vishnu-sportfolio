Medbridge

Boosting Workplace Wellbeing, Reducing Healthcare Provider Burnout

Introduction

Medbridge (MB), a healthcare IT company dedicated to meeting the evolving needs of its clients, sought to address Provider Burnout—an issue increasingly impacting healthcare workers, especially in light of the COVID-19 pandemic. This case study outlines how Koru collaborated with MB to drive a discovery initiative for building a Mental Health solution tailored to healthcare enterprises.

Background

The COVID-19 pandemic added immense stress to healthcare providers and clinicians, resulting in increased role overload, conflicts, work stress, and decreased mental health. MB recognized an opportunity to develop a solution to:
a. Provide practices visibility into employees' mental health status, enabling early intervention.
b. Empower clinicians to seek help and manage their stress transparently.

Problem Statement

The pandemic's unpredictability disrupted work routines, causing role overload, conflicts, and work stress for Primary Healthcare Workers. Healthcare professionals urgently needed a way to reduce workplace stress quickly to avoid its negative impacts on their physical and personal lives.

Recent Events and Mental Health

The pandemic, economic crises, and social movements like Black Lives Matter highlighted mental health concerns, especially among marginalized communities. Increased anxiety and upheaval manifested both physically and psychologically, necessitating sensitive approaches in mental health apps. These apps collect sensitive data on conditions like anxiety disorders, depression, bipolar disorders, eating disorders, and post-traumatic stress disorders. However, concerns arose due to data leaks and questionable data-sharing practices, exacerbating social stigmatization and hindering future opportunities.

Key Statistics

  • • 1 in 5 employees experiences moderate to high levels of stress.
  • • 48% of employees have experienced poor mental health in their daily lives.
  • • Every $1 invested in mental health support returns $4 in improved health and productivity.
  • • 63% of workers are ready to quit their jobs due to stress.
  • • Seeking therapy is expensive and carries a social stigma.

  • • Thorough competitor research to understand market needs.
  • • Mapping user personas aligned with business objectives.

  • • Implementing user progress tracking.

The Ignition Workshop:

This workshop involved exercises and activities to identify value propositions and generate ideas, with stakeholder and end-user involvement. Tools like Miro aided in efficient collaboration and idea generation. Through discussions and tools like Patient Health Questionnaire (PHQ-9) and Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD-7) assessments, problem statements emerged:

  • • Building a sense of belonging among clinicians.
  • • Identifying clinicians at risk of burnout.
  • • Establishing a foundation for psychological safety in the workplace.
  • • Creating awareness around mental health assistance.
  • • Empowering self-diagnosis and self-treatment.
  • • Simplifying access to mental health resources.
  • • Enabling users to find the right therapist.
